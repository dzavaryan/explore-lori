A Regional Chronicle

Explore Lori — An Independent Voice for Northern Armenia

An independent editorial platform documenting the heritage, landscape, and future of the Lori region. Not a guidebook. Not a booking engine. A chronicle.

Explore the Region →

Explore Lori is an independent initiative born from a simple observation: the story of Northern Armenia is too deep for a standard guidebook. Our mission is to act as a digital archive and a narrative stage for one of the world's most vertically diverse regions.

We are not a travel agency. We are not a commercial tour operator. We are a collection of researchers, storytellers, and residents dedicated to the Unseen Lori. We believe that by documenting the hidden theaters, the hand-carved trails, and the ancient stone architecture of our canyons, we can preserve the soul of this region for the next generation of explorers.

The story of Lori spans three millennia — from Urartian fortresses and UNESCO-listed monastery complexes, through the industrial copper towns of the Soviet canyon, to the COAF SMART Centers and Tumo Labs now reshaping village life at altitude. This platform exists to connect those three timelines into a single, living document.

Our Editorial Desks

Three pillars. One chronicle.

The Archive · Ancient

Stones & Centuries

We provide deep-dive appreciation pieces on the 3,000-year architectural history of the Debed and Dzoraget canyons — from UNESCO cathedrals to forgotten cliffside chapels accessible only on foot. Every wall has a date. We find it.

The Ledger · Hidden

The Unseen Lori

We curate the Lori Ledger — a community-sourced database of hidden viewpoints, local legends, and artisan traditions across Lori's 50+ villages. If it exists but is not documented, it is in danger of being lost. We document it.

The Horizon · Future

The Lori Renaissance

We document the modern educational initiatives and innovative regional developments transforming village life — COAF, Tumo, the next generation of highland farmers who are also coders. The future of Lori is not separate from its past.

Independence

No hidden incentives

No paid placements, no affiliate commissions, no sponsored content. If something is listed, it is because we believe it is worth your time — or worth preserving.

Accuracy

Depth over volume

Twelve well-described trails are more useful than 80 poorly researched ones. Our content is walked, verified, and written by people who have been to the place.

Benefit

Local communities first

The people who should gain most from increased visibility of Lori are the people who live here. Every editorial decision is made with that in mind.

Neutrality

An open directory

We list guides, guesthouses, and local experts that meet our editorial standards — regardless of affiliation. We celebrate local providers; we do not compete with them.

Open Ecosystem

An Open Platform for the Lori Region

Explore Lori is built on the philosophy of the open ecosystem. We do not compete with local providers — we celebrate them. We are actively seeking partnerships with organisations and individuals working to improve, document, or protect the Lori region.

Appreciation Journalism

How we write

Our content is driven by appreciation journalism. We don't just list a location — we spend time in its silence. Every story on this platform is a result of walking the trails, talking to the shepherds, and sitting in the stone haylofts of the Akner and Dsegh highlands.

We use the first-person plural — we — because this chronicle is not the work of one person. It belongs to everyone who has contributed a photograph, a trail note, a village name that was about to be forgotten. We invite you to add yours.
