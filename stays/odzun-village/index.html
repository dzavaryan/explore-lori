Odzun Village Stay

Odzun village
200 m from Odzun Basilica
From €25 / night
Dinner included

Odzun sits above the Debed valley on a wide plateau, high enough that the canyon is visible but you're not in it. The village has around 600 people, a functioning school, a bread oven at the edge of the main road, and one of the most quietly remarkable early Christian basilicas in the Caucasus — 6th century, tufa stone, carved reliefs on the southern facade that scholars have been arguing about since the 19th century. It's 200 metres from the front door of this guesthouse.

The family have been hosting travellers informally for years — before there was a platform for it, before anyone used the word guesthouse. What changed is that they made it more deliberate: two rooms prepared properly, the garden cleared, a proper bathroom. What didn't change is that you are staying with a family, not in a managed property. There's a difference. You can feel it by the second morning.

Dinner is included and it is cooked by Anahit, who has a view on every ingredient. The tomatoes come from the garden. The lavash is made in the village. The herbs are what's in season. In summer this means a table of green things — tarragon, basil, coriander — alongside grilled aubergine and whatever the garden produced that morning. In autumn, the walnuts arrive. In spring, it is dandelion salad and white cheese and something that tastes faintly of the forest. You won't go home without the recipe for at least one thing.

The guest rooms face the garden and the mountains behind Odzun. There is no television, which you'll stop noticing by the second day. There is wifi. The evenings are very quiet — the kind of quiet where you can hear the frogs in the irrigation ditch, which is a good sound.

What Odzun gives you access to

The Odzun Basilica is the obvious one — it's also the most undervisited significant church in the Debed corridor, which means you can spend half an hour there in near-solitude. Kobayr Monastery is 8 km by road, less through the forest, and worth the effort for the Georgian-Armenian frescoes that have been slowly returning from the plaster for the last decade. Haghpat and Sanahin are 20–25 km away. From Odzun the drives are mostly plateau roads, which is a relief after the canyon switchbacks.

Practical notes

  • Booking: write to hello@explorelori.com. Mention Odzun Village Stay. Anahit will write back.
  • Two guest rooms, maximum 4 guests. Not possible to book the property exclusively — it's a family home.
  • Dinner included every evening. Breakfast available for 2,000 AMD extra, or walk to the village bakery — both are fine options.
  • Getting there: marshrutka from Alaverdi to Odzun runs several times daily (roughly 45 minutes). Taxi from Alaverdi is around 3,000 AMD. Car is easiest — the road is good.
  • Cash only. Nearest ATM is in Alaverdi.
  • No pets.

Things worth knowing

  • Tell Anahit if you have dietary restrictions when you book. She will adapt — but she needs notice.
  • The basilica is best in early morning light, before tour groups arrive from Alaverdi.
  • Ask about the carved relief panels on the southern facade. There's a dispute about what they depict. Take a side.
  • Odzun plateau walks are unmarked but easy to navigate. The family can point you in the right direction.
