Tashir Highland Camp

Tashir region
1,800 m elevation
From €60 / night
Solar powered · Off-grid

Most of Lori's visitors stay in the canyon. The Tashir plateau is what's above it — an open highland at 1,700–1,900 metres where the landscape changes completely. The gorges flatten into meadows. The treeline drops away. The sky takes up more of your field of vision. Tashir Highland Camp sits at the northern edge of this plateau, on private land shared with a summer cattle farm, and it has some of the best unmediated views in northern Armenia.

The camp runs eight canvas tents on timber platforms. They are heated, because at 1,800 metres even July nights require it. The beds are real beds with proper linen, not sleeping bags on foam. The bathrooms are in a separate structure — composting toilets, solar-heated showers — and they work. The whole camp runs on a solar array that also powers the kitchen. No generator noise. When the wind drops in the evening, the silence is total except for the cattle moving in the pasture beyond the fence.

Breakfast is included and is cooked on a wood-fired stove — bread, eggs, local honey, whatever the camp has that morning. Dinner can be arranged with 24 hours notice, and it is worth arranging: lamb from the farm, herbs from the meadow, cooked over fire. There is a communal fire pit where people end up most evenings regardless of whether they planned to.

The stars here are the other reason people come. Tashir has minimal light pollution and sits at an altitude where the atmosphere is thinner. On clear nights — which is most nights in July and August — the Milky Way is not the vague smear you see from most places. It has structure. Photographers book several nights for this alone.

What the plateau gives you access to

Tashir Lake is 4 km from camp — a high-altitude lake that forms each spring from snowmelt and is surrounded by wildflower meadows in June and July. The Pambak Ridge traverse begins nearby: a hard multi-day route that crosses into the adjacent Tavush region. For something gentler, the plateau itself rewards slow walking — the variety of highland wildflowers in early summer is something botanists have been studying here for decades. Horse riding can be arranged through the farm.

Practical notes

  • Open June 1 to September 30 only. The access road is impassable in winter and unreliable in spring.
  • Booking is essential — write to hello@explorelori.com with your dates. Minimum stay is two nights; one night is too short to get the rhythm of the plateau.
  • Eight tents, maximum 16 guests. Possible to book the entire camp for groups.
  • Getting there: the camp is 14 km from Tashir town on an unpaved road. A 4x4 or high-clearance vehicle is strongly recommended. Alternatively, camp transport can be arranged from Tashir (4,000 AMD per vehicle).
  • Mobile signal is non-existent. Satellite wifi is available at the communal area for emergencies; it is not fast enough for streaming and we don't apologise for that.
  • Bring layers for evenings regardless of the daytime forecast. Temperature drops of 15°C between afternoon and midnight are not unusual.
  • Horse riding: half-day rides available, arrange at booking. The farm horses know the plateau routes.

Things worth knowing

  • Pack for 10°C colder than you expect. Every year someone arrives in sandals.
  • The fire pit is communal and usually lit by 8 PM. Bring something to share if you're that kind of person.
  • The access road is unpaved and has one river crossing that is passable in a standard car in late summer, less so in June. When in doubt, take the camp transport.
  • Cash only at camp — there are no card facilities. The nearest ATM is in Tashir town.
