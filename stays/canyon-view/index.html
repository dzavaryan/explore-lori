Canyon View Hotel

Stepanavan
Trail access from back door
From €50 / night
Restaurant on site

Stepanavan is the largest town in the northern part of Lori — around 15,000 people, a Soviet-era centre, and one of those Armenian towns that is more interesting than it looks from the main road. Canyon View Hotel sits at its eastern edge, where the town runs out and the Dzoraget gorge begins. That proximity is the point.

The renovation happened in 2023 — twelve rooms stripped back to concrete and rebuilt with something resembling restraint. Dark wood, local stone on the bathroom floors, good mattresses. The details that usually get skipped — blackout curtains that actually work, water pressure, USB charging points at the bed — were not skipped. It functions as a hotel, which sounds like a low bar but in northern Lori is worth saying explicitly.

The restaurant serves Armenian food with a menu that changes by season. In autumn this means pomegranate and walnut appearing in things you wouldn't expect them. The wine list is small and all Armenian. The coffee is from a roaster in Yerevan. Breakfast runs until 10 AM, which is considerate of them given that most people are here to do things that require early starts.

The back door opens onto the gorge trail. This is literally true — there is a gate in the fence at the back of the garden, and from there it is a short descent to the Dzoraget river path. The Dzoraget Gorge Loop is 12 km from this door. The Stepanavan Dendropark — one of the oldest arboretums in the Caucasus, 35 hectares of trees brought from six continents in the 1930s — is 2 km away on foot.

What Stepanavan gives you access to

The Dzoraget canyon is the obvious answer. Less obvious: Lori Berd, the medieval Armenian fortress 6 km south of the city, sitting above the confluence of the Dzoraget and Urut rivers, largely unvisited except by locals and the occasional determined traveller. The Dendropark in spring is extraordinary — the sequoias are the ones people photograph, but the variety goes much deeper than that. Dsegh, Tumanyan's village, is 18 km away.

Practical notes

  • Booking: book directly via hello@explorelori.com or the hotel's own page (link coming). We always recommend booking direct — it's cheaper and you get a real response.
  • 12 rooms. Bikes available to rent from reception — useful for the gorge roads and the Dendropark loop.
  • Restaurant serves dinner until 10 PM. Reservation recommended in summer and on weekends.
  • Stepanavan is accessible by marshrutka from Yerevan (around 3 hours) and from Vanadzor (1 hour). The hotel can arrange taxi pickup.
  • Parking on site. The road from the M6 highway is straightforward.

Things worth knowing

  • The gorge-facing rooms are worth paying for. Ask for rooms 8–12 when booking.
  • The Dzoraget trail from the back gate is unmarked for the first kilometre. The hotel can give you a hand-drawn map.
  • Stepanavan has ATMs, a pharmacy, and a good market on Fridays. Stock up here before heading to smaller villages.
  • Book the bike rental the evening before — there are only four of them.
